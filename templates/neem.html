Neem (வேப்பம்)

SCIENTIFIC NAME: Azadirachta Indica

TAMIL NAME: வேப்பம் (Vembu)

PARTS USED: Leaf, Bark, Flower, Fruit (Neem Oil), Twig

KEY TRADITIONAL PROPERTIES

  • Veppam is a major herb in Siddha Medicine, famous for its Kasam (bitter taste) and ability to balance excess Pitta and Kapha doshas.
  • Anti-Pyretic: Helps reduce all types of fevers (e.g., intermittent and chronic fevers).
  • Janthu Hara: Strong anti-parasitic and anti-helminthic action (kills worms).
  • Vrana Ropana: Promotes rapid wound healing.
  • Kushtagna: Highly effective against various skin diseases (leprosy, eczema, fungal infections).

TRADITIONAL USES, DISEASES CURED, & APPLICATION

1. Skin Diseases (Eczema, Psoriasis, Fungal Infections)

  • Cure/Relief: Its anti-bacterial and anti-fungal properties cleanse the skin and reduce inflammation.
  • Application: Paste of Neem leaves is applied externally to affected areas. Consuming a small quantity of Neem leaf juice or capsules helps purify the blood from within.

2. Fever & Malaria

  • Cure/Relief: Acts as a natural antipyretic and its constituents are effective against malarial parasites.
  • Application: Decoction (Kashayam) made from Neem bark or leaves is traditionally consumed to lower fever and combat the infection.

3. Dental & Oral Health

  • Cure/Relief: Prevents gum disease, tooth decay, and bad breath due to its antiseptic nature.
  • Application: Chewing a fresh Neem twig (Datun) is a historical and effective method for brushing teeth and strengthening gums.

4. Diabetes Management

  • Cure/Relief: Certain compounds in Neem leaves are known to help lower and regulate blood sugar levels.
  • Application: Neem leaf powder or extract is taken orally daily, under the guidance of a traditional practitioner.

5. Wound & Ulcer Healing

  • Cure/Relief: Accelerates the healing process by keeping the wound free of infection and reducing inflammation.
  • Application: Neem oil or powdered dried leaves are sprinkled/applied directly onto wounds, cuts, or chronic ulcers.
